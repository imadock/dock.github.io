About

I am a junior at the University of California, San Diego pursuing a B.S. in Biochemistry. I currently conduct research in the Engle Lab at the Scripps Research Institute, where I perform multi-step organic synthesis of small molecule drug candidates using transition metal-catalyzed methods.

My research interests lie at the intersection of organic chemistry and medicinal chemistry, with a focus on developing efficient synthetic routes to biologically active molecules. I am also interested in molecular biology, having recently completed a project cloning the bioluminescent lux operon from Aliivibrio fischeri into E. coli.

I speak English, Mandarin Chinese, and Japanese, and am interested in pursuing graduate studies in chemistry.
